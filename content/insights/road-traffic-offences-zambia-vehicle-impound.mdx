The Road Transport and Safety Agency (RTSA) has statutory authority to detain
motor vehicles in certain circumstances. Drivers, fleet operators, and logistics
businesses frequently encounter impound notices and are unsure when the action
is lawful and when it is not.

## When may a vehicle be impounded?

Common grounds include:

- Driving without a valid licence or fitness certificate.
- Operating a public service vehicle without the appropriate licensing.
- Driving under the influence.
- Causing an accident that requires forensic examination of the vehicle.

## What to do if your vehicle is impounded

1. Request a written notice from the impounding officer.
2. Verify the asserted offence against the underlying section of the Road
   Traffic Act.
3. Engage counsel early — impound durations beyond seven days may be challenged.

## Practical guidance for businesses

Operators with vehicle fleets should keep a single point of contact responsible
for impound notices, retain copies of all vehicle documentation off-site, and
ensure drivers know how to obtain and preserve impound receipts.
