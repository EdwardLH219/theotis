The Government of the Republic of Zambia has tabled a draft Alternative Dispute
Resolution Bill that, if enacted, will substantially modernise the framework
for arbitration, mediation, and adjudication in the country.

For businesses operating in Zambia, the proposed reforms speak directly to three
recurring frustrations: the time taken to resolve commercial disputes, the
predictability of enforcement, and the cost of cross-border arbitration when
seated locally.

## What the Bill addresses

- A consolidated statutory basis for mediation, arbitration, and adjudication.
- Clarity on the enforcement of foreign arbitral awards.
- A more codified role for institutional bodies, including the Lusaka
  International Arbitration Centre (LIAC).

## Why it matters for commercial parties

For our clients, the practical effects fall into three categories:

1. Drafting — dispute-resolution clauses in commercial contracts will benefit
   from a fresh review against the new framework.
2. Live disputes — parties already in arbitration should consider whether to
   accelerate or wait on procedural questions that may be clarified by the new
   Act.
3. Enforcement — the position of foreign award-holders looking to enforce in
   Zambia will likely improve.

We will continue to track the Bill through Parliament and publish updates as
the text evolves.
